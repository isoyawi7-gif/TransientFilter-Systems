Introduction

Telecommunications applications in Papua New Guinea is frequently affected by power instability, blackouts, and lightning strikes. During transitions between power sources, surge currents damage sensitive components, causing failures and downtime.

This research focuses on improving surge protection, grounding, and earthing systems to enhance applications reliability and reduce operational risks.

Problem Statement

Telecommunication base station equipment is highly vulnerable to electrical surges caused by lightning strikes, unstable grid supply, and switching transients. In many installations, especially in regions with high lightning activity and unreliable power infrastructure, existing surge protection systems are either inadequate, poorly designed, or improperly maintained.

As a result, these surges lead to frequent equipment failures, increased operational downtime, and high maintenance and replacement costs. Despite the availability of surge protection devices, there is limited localized research on their effectiveness under real operating conditions and how they can be optimized for improved protection.

Therefore, there is a need to investigate the causes and impact of electrical surges on telecom systems and to develop a more reliable and efficient surge protection approach suitable for such environments.

Research Objectives

  • Mitigate equipment stalling during steady-state operation
  • Improve surge protection and earthing systems
  • Capture and analyze overcurrent events
  • Ensure long-term system reliability
